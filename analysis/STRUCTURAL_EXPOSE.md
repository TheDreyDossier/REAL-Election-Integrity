# The DOJ "Election Integrity" Dashboard: How 4 Items Became 243

**A structural analysis of civilrights.justice.gov/electionintegrity**

*Version 1.0 — July 16, 2026*

---

## The single sentence

**The DOJ's new "Election Integrity" dashboard displays 51 completed enforcement actions dated September 18, 2026 — a date that is nine weeks in the future.**

That is not a typo. It is the structural design of the site.

---

## What the dashboard shows

Launched July 16, 2026 at [civilrights.justice.gov/electionintegrity](https://civilrights.justice.gov/electionintegrity), the site presents itself as a real-time map of federal election-integrity enforcement. It reports:

- **243 "verified enforcement actions"**
- Across **all 50 states plus D.C.**
- All attributed to the **Department of Justice**
- Each state's page shows dated, individually-numbered actions

The visual language — a D3 choropleth map, per-state dossiers, action counts, a "Report a Violation" button — is designed to convey scale, momentum, and rigor.

## What is actually there

Of the 243 entries, **156 (64%)** are duplicates of just **four underlying items**, each replicated once per state:

| Template | Rows on dashboard | What it actually is |
|---|---:|---|
| **"Warns State Officials of Criminal Liability"** (dated 7/7/2026) | **51** | One letter, identical language, signed by Asst. AG Harmeet Dhillon, sent to all 51 chief election officials on the same day |
| **"Ensures Primary Election Military Ballots"** (various 2026 dates) | **51** | The routine annual UOCAVA compliance-monitoring program DOJ has run every federal election cycle since 1986 |
| **"Ensures General Election Military Ballots"** (dated 9/18/2026) | **51** | The same routine program — displayed today as completed, dated 64 days in the future |
| **"Withdrew from Biden-Era Race-Based Election Maps"** (AL, LA, TX) | **3** | Withdrawals from 7 total Voting Rights Act cases; the Alabama entry links to a voter-roll purge case, not a redistricting case |
| **TOTAL** | **156 of 243** | **4 real items** |

The remaining 87 entries include a small number of sourced individual claims and a much larger set of unsourced ones. **Only 32 of 243 entries (13.2%) link to any source document at all.**

## The four structural problems

### 1. Future-dated enforcement

Fifty-one entries are dated **September 18, 2026** — the statutory deadline under 52 U.S.C. § 20302(a)(8)(A) by which states must mail absentee ballots to military and overseas voters (exactly 45 days before Election Day). It is a deadline states are required by law to meet. It has not yet arrived.

The dashboard displays these entries today, July 16, 2026, as **verified, completed federal enforcement actions**.

This is the single cleanest structural fact: an event that has not happened is presented as an event that has been accomplished.

### 2. Category error: routine compliance as enforcement

The Uniformed and Overseas Citizens Absentee Voting Act of 1986 (UOCAVA), amended by the MOVE Act of 2009, requires DOJ's Civil Rights Division Voting Section to monitor UOCAVA compliance in every state, in every federal election cycle. DOJ's own annual reports to Congress — [2010](https://www.justice.gov/sites/default/files/crt/legacy/2011/01/13/move_act_report.pdf), [2013](https://www.justice.gov/sites/default/files/crt/legacy/2014/01/09/2013_uocava_report.pdf), 2014, and every year since — document the same pattern: send letters to all covered jurisdictions in the spring, monitor transmission, take enforcement action only where a state fails to comply.

In DOJ's own [March 2026 letter to California](https://www.democracydocket.com/wp-content/uploads/2026/03/CA-DOJ-UOCAVA-1.pdf) (reported by [Democracy Docket](https://www.democracydocket.com/news-alerts/trump-doj-expands-crackdown-on-overseas-voters-over-id/)):

> "*Pursuant to our UOCAVA enforcement authority, we will request, **as we have in prior election cycles**, that your office monitor the transmission of absentee ballots...*"

**"As we have in prior election cycles"** is DOJ's own language for what this program is. The Biden DOJ ran the same program for the 2024 cycle, [reaching agreements with New York, Kansas, and Mississippi](https://www.justice.gov/archives/opa/pr/justice-department-reached-agreements-protect-rights-military-and-overseas-voters-new-york) in February 2025.

Routine, statutorily-mandated, bipartisan-continuity compliance work is being displayed as 102 discrete Trump-era enforcement wins.

### 3. Count inflation: one letter as 51 actions

On July 7, 2026, DOJ sent an identical letter to the chief election officials of all 50 states plus D.C., warning of potential criminal liability for allowing noncitizens to remain on voter rolls. The story was covered the same day by [Reuters](https://www.reuters.com/legal/government/us-justice-department-tells-state-officials-they-could-be-prosecuted-over-2026-07-08/), [Votebeat](https://www.votebeat.org/national/2026/07/07/trump-department-justice-letter-noncitizens-voter-rolls-election-officials/), [ABC News](https://abcnews.com/US/ahead-midterms-doj-warns-state-officials-potential-criminal/story?id=134566080), [CBS News](https://www.cbsnews.com/news/justice-department-letter-noncitizen-voter-rolls-criminal-liability-warning/), and [NOTUS](https://www.notus.org/2026-election/doj-threat-election-officials).

[Votebeat's reporting](https://www.votebeat.org/national/2026/07/07/trump-department-justice-letter-noncitizens-voter-rolls-election-officials/) found the letter was delivered to some states through generic public-facing email inboxes — the kind used to route citizen inquiries. It was a mass communication, not 51 individually-tailored investigations.

The dashboard converts one letter into 51 numbered "enforcement actions."

Substantively, the letter's premise is contested: noncitizen voting has been a federal crime since IIRIRA in 1996, empirical evidence of it remains exceedingly rare, and [CBS's reporting](https://www.cbsnews.com/news/justice-department-letter-noncitizen-voter-rolls-criminal-liability-warning/) notes that "current safeguards are effective at preventing illegal registration and voting."

### 4. Mischaracterization: what "withdrew from Biden-era race maps" actually means

Three states — Alabama, Louisiana, and Texas — carry entries claiming DOJ "Withdrew from Biden-Era Suit Demanding Race-Based Election Maps."

The claim is misleading in three ways:

- **The Alabama entry links to the wrong case.** DOJ's own [press release](https://www.justice.gov/opa/pr/us-department-justice-dismisses-biden-era-lawsuit-against-alabama-order-have-more-secure) — linked directly from the dashboard — describes dismissal of a **voter roll purge case**, not a race-based redistricting case. The dashboard's summary contradicts its own linked source.
- **There are seven, not three.** According to the [Brennan Center](https://www.brennancenter.org/our-work/analysis-opinion/justice-department-shirking-its-responsibility-voters), DOJ has withdrawn from **seven** Section 2 Voting Rights Act cases. The dashboard displays three. The other four are omitted.
- **"Biden-era" obscures the legal context.** In *Louisiana v. Callais*, [Democracy Docket reports](https://www.democracydocket.com/news-alerts/once-the-voting-rights-acts-champion-doj-now-wants-scotus-to-gut-it/) DOJ did not merely withdraw — it filed a new brief on September 25, 2025, arguing Section 2 of the Voting Rights Act is unconstitutional. The Supreme Court's April 2026 decision in *Callais* ([Wikipedia summary](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Louisiana_v._Callais)) did not, as the dashboard implies, categorically rule race-based maps unconstitutional. In dissent, Justice Kagan wrote that the majority "renders Section 2 all but a dead letter."

The dashboard reframes DOJ's active effort to weaken the Voting Rights Act as a defensive correction of Biden-era overreach.

## Why this matters

A public-facing federal enforcement dashboard, distributed under the Department of Justice's civil rights domain, is the highest-credibility surface the executive branch has for representing what its law enforcement is doing. When that surface displays:

- future events as accomplished,
- routine statutory work as discretionary wins,
- one letter as 51 investigations,
- and reframes Voting Rights Act retreat as courtroom victory,

...it converts an oversight instrument into a campaign instrument. The distinction matters because voters, reporters, election administrators, and courts rely on federal enforcement reporting to distinguish real activity from claimed activity ahead of a midterm.

The four items are the story. The 243 count is the frame.
