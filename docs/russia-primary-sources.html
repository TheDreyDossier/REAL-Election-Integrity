Russia 2016 — Trump's own conduct, cited to the page

The current administration is publishing rhetorical claims that the 2016 Russia investigation was a "hoax." The actual government record — the Mueller Report, the Senate Intelligence Committee's Republican-chaired Volume 5, the Intelligence Community Assessment, and the federal grand-jury indictments of Russian officers — remains in the public record.

Every row in the tables below links directly to the specific page of the original document that shows Donald Trump's own conduct: welcoming, encouraging, or personally participating in the effort. No excerpts. No rewrites. The government's own pages, opened to the exact citation.

How to read this page. Click any page button and your browser will open the source PDF directly to that page. The gold-highlighted rows are the five citations that stand alone as evidence of Trump's personal conduct — start there if you only have a few minutes.

Mueller Report (Volumes I & II)

Department of Justice, March 2019 · 448 pages · Open full PDF
PageWhat Trump personally did
p. 57 key Trump publicly asks Russia to hack Clinton. July 27, 2016: "Russia, if you're listening, I hope you're able to find the 30,000 emails that are missing. I think you will probably be rewarded mightily by our press." The same day, GRU officers launched new spearphishing attacks against Clinton's personal office.
p. 61-62 Trump is in the room when a WikiLeaks release is previewed. Cohen witnesses Trump on speakerphone with Roger Stone being told a release is coming. Trump then tells Rick Gates in the car that "more releases of damaging information would be coming."
p. 74-86 Trump was pursuing a Moscow real-estate deal while running for President. Trump personally authorized Michael Cohen to pursue the Trump Tower Moscow project, with contact into Putin's office (via Peskov). He then publicly denied it.
p. 118-121 key The June 9, 2016 Trump Tower meeting. Don Jr. is offered damaging Clinton material framed as "part of Russia and its government's support for Mr. Trump." He replies "if it's what you say I love it." Manafort and Kushner attend the meeting.
p. 217 key Trump personally edited the false press statement about the meeting. He deleted the line acknowledging the meeting was about damaging information on Clinton — leaving only the "Russian adoption" cover story.
p. 230-231 Volume II obstruction analysis: Trump's written answers claim "Russia if you're listening" was said "in jest." Mueller's assessment: no.
p. 316 Trump directed Hope Hicks to publicly describe the June 9 meeting as being "about Russian adoption" — a story he knew was false.
p. 351 Cohen submitted false testimony to Congress about the Moscow project "in accordance with the party line" — coordinated with Trump.
p. 421 Written questions submitted to Trump about his knowledge of the July 27 statement, the WikiLeaks contacts, and the hack — with Trump's evasive answers reproduced verbatim.

Senate Intelligence Committee, Volume 5

Republican-chaired committee (Chairman Marco Rubio) · Unanimous · August 2020 · 966 pages · Open full PDF
PageWhat Trump personally did
p. 187 key The bipartisan Senate committee's own finding: "Trump also directed individuals in, and associated with, his Campaign to seek out Clinton's 'missing' emails." This is not a Democratic report. This is a Republican-chaired committee stating it as fact.
p. 246 The "Russia, if you're listening" statement in the committee's own hack-and-leak analysis.
p. 251-258 Trump's phone calls with Roger Stone during the WikiLeaks release period. Stone reported to Gates that "Trump asked [Stone] for some other things" Stone was "writing now."
p. 263 Committee finding: Trump called Stone on Keith Schiller's phone specifically about a coming WikiLeaks release.
p. 269-271 key Trump publicly praised WikiLeaks 160+ times in the last month of the campaign. Quoted verbatim: "WikiLeaks, I love WikiLeaks." After his public praise on October 10, WikiLeaks resumed messaging Trump Jr.
p. 361-371 The committee's parallel record of the June 9 Trump Tower meeting, including Don Jr.'s "I love it especially later in the summer" reply and Trump's own June 7 rally promising a "major speech" on Clinton.
p. 444 Cohen testifies that Sater said Putin was personally aware of the Trump Tower Moscow deal — while Trump publicly promised a "very good relationship with Putin."
p. 792-797 Peter Smith operation: Trump associates sought Clinton's "missing" emails from people they believed to be Russian hackers, with Trump publicizing his interest.
