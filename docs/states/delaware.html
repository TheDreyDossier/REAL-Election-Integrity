Delaware

DOJ's dashboard lists 4 enforcement actions for Delaware. Below is every entry verbatim, with an inline fact-check on each claim that falls into the four structural templates. DOJ's own page for Delaware: https://www.justice.gov/crt/election-integrity-delaware.

  1. 12/2/2025 DOJ
    DOJ Sued Delaware for Failing to Provide Federal Election Records
    Fact Check Unverified — pending Tier 2 review
    This individual claim is not part of the four structural templates. It is being reviewed against primary sources (docket filings, press releases, state responses). Status will be updated here as verification completes.
  2. 7/7/2026 DOJ
    DOJ Warns State Officials of Their Criminal Liability Regarding Illegal Voting
    Fact Check Count inflation
    This is one letter, signed by Assistant Attorney General Harmeet Dhillon on July 7, 2026 and sent the same day to all 51 chief state election officials with identical language. It is being counted as 51 separate enforcement actions on this dashboard. Votebeat found the letter was delivered to some states through generic public inboxes, not tailored investigations.
    Sources: Reuters · Votebeat · ABC News · NOTUS · Full memo →
  3. 7/27/2026 (FUTURE-DATED) DOJ
    DOJ Ensures Primary Election Military Ballots Are Sent Early to Comply With Federal Law
    Fact Check Routine work reframed
    This is routine annual UOCAVA compliance monitoring, a program the DOJ Civil Rights Division has run every federal election cycle since 1986 in every state under the Uniformed and Overseas Citizens Absentee Voting Act. In DOJ's own March 2026 letter to California: “Pursuant to our UOCAVA enforcement authority, we will request, as we have in prior election cycles, that your office monitor the transmission of absentee ballots.” The Biden DOJ ran the same program for the 2024 cycle.
    Sources: DOJ CRT — UOCAVA overview · DOJ 2010 UOCAVA report to Congress · Democracy Docket — Trump DOJ 2026 letter · Biden DOJ 2024 UOCAVA agreements · Full memo →
  4. 9/18/2026 (FUTURE-DATED) DOJ
    DOJ Ensures General Election Military Ballots Are Sent Early to Comply With Federal Law
    Fact Check Future-dated as accomplished
    This entry is dated September 18, 2026 — the statutory 45-day deadline under 52 U.S.C. § 20302(a)(8)(A) by which states must mail absentee ballots to military and overseas voters. That date is after the DOJ dashboard launched. The event has not yet occurred, but is displayed today as a completed, verified federal enforcement action. Compliance is a state obligation; DOJ's role is monitoring.
    Sources: 52 U.S.C. § 20302 (UOCAVA) · DOJ CRT — UOCAVA overview · Federal Voting Assistance Program · Full memo →
