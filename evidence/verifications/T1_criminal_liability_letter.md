# Template Verification #1 — "DOJ Warns State Officials of Their Criminal Liability Regarding Illegal Voting"

## DOJ's Claim (Dashboard)
- **Appears in:** all 51 states + DC
- **Date shown:** 7/7/2026 (in every state)
- **Displayed as:** 51 separate "verified enforcement actions"
- **DOJ description:** "DOJ notified state officials of their duty to prevent illegal voting. DOJ reminded them that it is a crime to intentionally allow ineligible persons to vote or conspire to interfere with another person's constitutional rights."
- **Source link on DOJ page:** NONE

## Verified Reality (CONFIRMED)

**This is ONE letter, sent by ONE person, on ONE day, to all 50 states + DC — displayed on the dashboard as 51 discrete enforcement actions.**

### Primary facts
- **Letters were dated Tuesday, July 7, 2026**
- **Signed by Harmeet Dhillon**, Assistant Attorney General for Civil Rights ([Reuters](https://www.reuters.com/legal/government/us-justice-department-tells-state-officials-they-could-be-prosecuted-over-2026-07-08/))
- **Sent to all 50 states + D.C.** (originally announced as targeting "at least 14 states" per [Votebeat](https://www.votebeat.org/national/2026/07/07/trump-department-justice-letter-noncitizens-voter-rolls-election-officials/), later confirmed by DOJ spokesperson as all 50 + DC)
- **The letters contained identical language** across states ([Votebeat: "At least 13 other states, including Arizona, Georgia, Kentucky, and Tennessee, received letters containing identical language"](https://www.votebeat.org/national/2026/07/07/trump-department-justice-letter-noncitizens-voter-rolls-election-officials/))
- **DOJ spokesperson confirmation:** "The Department sent these letters to all 50 states and the District of Columbia, asking for voluntary compliance in a timely manner with their obligations under federal law to ensure only citizens vote in federal elections" ([Reuters](https://www.reuters.com/legal/government/us-justice-department-tells-state-officials-they-could-be-prosecuted-over-2026-07-08/))

### The delivery method itself
- **[Votebeat](https://www.votebeat.org/national/2026/07/07/trump-department-justice-letter-noncitizens-voter-rolls-election-officials/):** "in several states, the Justice Department sent the letter to the generic email addresses listed on agency websites for use by the public."

**One boilerplate letter, sent to public-facing generic email inboxes, presented on a national dashboard as 51 discrete enforcement actions.**

### Broader context (from press coverage)
- Reuters characterizes this as "the latest instance of Trump administration pressuring states over elections" and notes it comes "despite [DOJ's] courtroom setbacks"
- NOTUS: "It's already illegal for noncitizens to vote under the Illegal Immigration Reform and Immigrant Responsibility Act of 1996. Any noncitizen who attempts to break the federal law could be fined, imprisoned for up to one year, or both."
- Multiple outlets note: **noncitizen voting is already illegal, already prosecuted, and empirically exceedingly rare.** The letter created no new law and pointed to no specific cases of noncitizens on rolls.
- ABC News: One election official who received a letter described it as "threatening."
- CBS News (via Facebook post): "Noncitizen voting in U.S. federal and state elections is very rare. Current safeguards are effective at preventing illegal registration and voting."

## Structural Assessment

**MISLEADING → SEVERELY MISLEADING**

The claim that this represents 51 "enforcement actions" is misleading in three ways:

1. **Count inflation.** One letter counted 51 times. The site's map shows a shaded state for each of the 51 "actions" — this is one action.
2. **"Enforcement" framing.** Sending a warning letter demanding a 5-day compliance response is not enforcement — no case was filed, no investigation opened, no violation established. It is a preemptive threat.
3. **Delivery via public inboxes.** In some states DOJ used the state's generic public contact email, meaning the "letter to the chief election officer" was in some cases indistinguishable from unsolicited email.
