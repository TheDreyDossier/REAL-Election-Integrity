## Verified Reality (CONFIRMED)

**This is UOCAVA compliance monitoring — a routine, statutorily-mandated, decades-old program that DOJ has run every election cycle since 1986 for every U.S. state and territory. Presenting it as "enforcement action" is a category error.**

### What UOCAVA actually is
- **The Uniformed and Overseas Citizens Absentee Voting Act (UOCAVA)** was passed in 1986 (52 U.S.C. § 20302 et seq.). It requires states to send absentee ballots to military and overseas voters at least 45 days before every federal election.
- **The MOVE Act of 2009** (Military and Overseas Voter Empowerment Act) added the 45-day mailing requirement and required the Attorney General to file an annual report to Congress on UOCAVA enforcement (52 U.S.C. § 20307(b)).
- **The Civil Rights Division's Voting Section has been assigned UOCAVA enforcement since 1986.** ([DOJ CRT — UOCAVA page](https://www.justice.gov/crt/uniformed-and-overseas-citizens-absentee-voting-act))
- **DOJ's own description of its work:** "Since UOCAVA was enacted in 1986, the Civil Rights Division has brought numerous enforcement lawsuits" ([DOJ CRT](https://www.justice.gov/crt/uniformed-and-overseas-citizens-absentee-voting-act))

### It happens every year, in every state — as routine work

From DOJ's own annual reports to Congress (a public series going back to 2010):

- **2010 UOCAVA Report to Congress ([DOJ PDF](https://www.justice.gov/sites/default/files/crt/legacy/2011/01/13/move_act_report.pdf))** — "In April 2010, the Department sent letters to all covered jurisdictions reminding them of the MOVE Act's requirements and requesting information about their plans for complying with the law."
- **2013 UOCAVA Report to Congress ([DOJ PDF](https://www.justice.gov/sites/default/files/crt/legacy/2014/01/09/2013_uocava_report.pdf))** — "As in prior Federal election cycles, the Department will contact all the chief State election officials to remind them of their UOCAVA responsibilities and to discuss their preparations for the 2024 Federal primary elections."
- **The 2024 pattern (Biden DOJ):** DOJ "Reached Agreements to Protect Rights of Military and Overseas Voters from New York, Kansas and Mississippi" ([DOJ archive, 2025-02-06](https://www.justice.gov/archives/opa/pr/justice-department-reached-agreements-protect-rights-military-and-overseas-voters-new-york))

### The 2026 cycle continues the identical pattern

- **Democracy Docket, 3/13/2026:** Quotes a DOJ letter from trial attorney Brittany E. Bennett: **"As part of our nationwide UOCAVA enforcement program, we are assessing states' efforts to ensure compliance in 2026."** ([Democracy Docket](https://www.democracydocket.com/news-alerts/trump-doj-expands-crackdown-on-overseas-voters-over-id/))
- **DOJ's own letter to California ([Democracy Docket PDF](https://www.democracydocket.com/wp-content/uploads/2026/03/CA-DOJ-UOCAVA-1.pdf))**: "Pursuant to our UOCAVA enforcement authority, we will request, as we have in prior election cycles, that your office monitor the transmission of absentee ballots to UOCAVA voters to ensure that ballots are sent by the State's local jurisdictions by the 45th-day deadline"

**DOJ's own words:** "*as we have in prior election cycles.*"

### The 9/18/2026 date is a statutory deadline, not an accomplishment

- **9/18/2026 = exactly 45 days before 11/3/2026** (Election Day, 2026 midterms)
- Under 52 U.S.C. § 20302(a)(8)(A), states must mail UOCAVA ballots by that date
- Every state has to do this every federal election cycle. It is not a DOJ enforcement action — it is a state statutory obligation. DOJ merely monitors compliance.
- On the dashboard, DOJ presents this future-dated statutory deadline as **51 accomplished federal enforcement actions**.

### Historical context — this is business as usual
- 2010: DOJ took action against 14 jurisdictions for UOCAVA violations (11 states, 2 territories, DC)
- 2013: 3 enforcement actions for special elections
- Every year since: annual pattern of letters → monitoring → occasional lawsuits for violations
- The Voting Section publishes an annual report of these actions to Congress every December

## Structural Assessment

**MISLEADING → CATEGORY ERROR + FUTURE-DATED**

Six structural problems:

1. **Routine work reframed as enforcement.** This is annual statutory compliance monitoring, not enforcement. Actual enforcement happens when a state *fails* to comply — those cases are numbered in the single digits in most years.
2. **Count inflation.** DOJ contacts all 50 states + DC in every cycle. Counting each state's routine compliance letter as a separate "enforcement action" is padding.
3. **Bipartisan continuity presented as Trump-era achievement.** UOCAVA enforcement has continued through every administration since 1986. The 2024 cycle under Biden's DOJ included the same activities.
4. **Future-dated events as accomplished actions.** 51 of the entries are dated 9/18/2026 — the statutory ballot-mailing deadline that is 64 days *after* the dashboard launched. States haven't yet complied — the deadline hasn't arrived.
5. **Missing context: state obligation, not federal action.** The 45-day deadline is a state obligation. DOJ's role is monitoring. Framing DOJ as "ensuring" this happens implies DOJ is doing the work, when the states are.
6. **No cited violation, no cited case.** For an ordinary DOJ enforcement claim, there would be a state that failed to comply, a case number, a court order. None exists for any of these 102 entries.

## Combined arithmetic

- **Templates 1 + 2 + 3 = 153 of 243 total dashboard entries (63%).**

  - 1 criminal-liability letter (dated 7/7/2026, one document)

  - 1 routine general-cycle UOCAVA monitoring program (future-dated to 9/18/2026)
- **Three items, presented as 153.**
